Corporate KPIs

Leading the Expansion

As the AWS AI Division Leader, you must rapidly scale infrastructure while managing complex local resistance. Your decisions balance the pressure for financial returns with corporate ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) commitments.

Deployment Speed & ROI

Measures how fast you can build the data center and the operational margins it produces. Standardized builds and utilizing existing fossil-fuel grids maximize this score. Bespoke redesigns and expensive renewables lower it.

Stakeholder Trust

Represents your "Social License to Operate." Transparency, robust Community Benefit Agreements (CBAs), and active listening improve your brand reputation. Strict NDAs and bypassing community concerns trigger backlash.

Env. Stewardship

Reflects your alignment with integral ecology and corporate ESG goals. Mandating clean energy, implementing closed-loop water systems, and protecting local landscapes drive this score higher. Exploiting local resources drives it down.

Regulatory Compliance

Measures protection against litigation, FOIA delays, and zoning appeals. Collaborating with town planners and accepting regulatory limits increases stability. Pushing legal boundaries risks costly project injunctions.

Executive Roleplay Instructions

The simulated chat mirrors the intense public resistance data centers face regarding environmental racism, opacity, and resource drain. You must now negotiate your Special Use Permit (SUP).

  • Step 1: Executive Huddle (10 mins) - Divide into assigned stakeholder groups. As the AWS Leadership Team, you must identify which margins you can compress to win public approval.
    • AWS / Data Services (You): Fight for standard industrial designs and immediate grid access. Try to offer direct fee-in-lieu payments to bypass complex school funding. Emphasize your role in global decarbonization modeling.
    • Town Council Majority: Demanding a massive revenue win but need political cover. They want a robust Community Benefit Agreement.
    • Citizens / Preservation Advocates: Demanding algorithmic restraint and zero fossil-fuel emissions to prevent fence-line pollution. They will threaten FOIA litigation if NDAs remain.
    • Town Planners / Utilities: Focused strictly on preventing regional grid strain, enforcing zoning aesthetics, and preventing aquifer depletion.
  • Step 2: Opening Statements (10 mins) - Each group gets 2 minutes. As AWS, set the tone: Are you aggressive disrupters or collaborative partners?
  • Step 3: Open Negotiation (25 mins) - Debate the core conditions: NDAs, Gas Turbines/Emissions, Architectural Caps, and Water Usage.
  • Step 4: The Final Vote (10 mins) - The Town Council votes. Will your project be approved, or have you failed to secure your social license to operate?
