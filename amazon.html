Decision KPIs

Evaluating the Project

As the Town Council, your job is to analyze this hyperscale project based on four distinct axes reflecting the community's primary concerns and the public record. Every decision is a valid trade-off between growth and preservation.

Fiscal Stability

Tracks the estimated $550M corporate investment, massive tax windfalls, secondary job spillovers, and potential funding for Community Benefit Agreements. High scores indicate a well-funded municipal budget; low scores indicate deficit risk or opportunity costs.

Public Trust / Legitimacy

Measures institutional confidence. Transparency, responsive processes, and active community engagement improve this score. NDAs, perceived backdoor deals, and closed records degrade it.

Gateway Compatibility

Reflects the physical impact on Warrenton's scenic character and the mitigation of environmental racism. Evaluates constraints on emissions, noise, massing limits, and water utilization against the global need for decarbonization infrastructure.

Legal Durability

Measures the Town's exposure to appeals, FOIA litigation, and vested-rights battles. Actions that overstep zoning authority or deny procedural rights drastically lower this score.
