The Income Statement

Question 4: The Reality Check

If a business reports a 1 million Galleon profit, do they actually have 1 million Galleons in the bank right now?

Why is Profit not the same as Cash?

Click to Reveal the Magic

Statement of Cash Flows

Absolutely not! You can sell a potion today and make a profit on paper, but if the customer promises to pay you next month, your actual cash hasn't changed yet.

✨ The Gringotts Vault Rule

The Cash Flow Statement completely ignores "promises." It only tracks real coins entering and leaving your vault through three magical doors:

Operating Daily shop sales and paying the usual bills.
Investing Buying or selling long-term gear, like cauldrons.
Financing Borrowing from Gringotts or owner investments.

The Harsh Truth

PROFIT ≠ CASH FLOW

Magical Cheat Sheet

The Balance Sheet

A snapshot of what the business owns and owes at a specific moment in time.

Assets = Liabilities + Equity

The Income Statement

A record of performance over a period of time to see if you made a profit.

Revenue - Expenses = Net Income

The Missing Link

Net Income flows directly into Equity, magically connecting the two statements.

Equity = Capital + Net Income

The Expanded Golden Rule

Assets = Liabilities + Capital + Revenue - Expenses
